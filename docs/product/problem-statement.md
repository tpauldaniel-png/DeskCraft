# DeskCraft Problem statement and value proposition

**Status:** Draft
**Date:** 21 July 2026

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the customer problem DeskCraft intends to address, the value the product proposes to provide, and the assumptions that must be validated. It keeps the product focused on customer needs before implementation decisions are made.

## 2. Target Customers:

- Primary Customer:  Software Developers 
- Secondary Customers: work from home users, freelancers, home-office users, students
- Relevant situation: For the past 5 years hybrid work culture and work from home became the norm, hence there is a need for workspace setup where they spend considerable amount of time in a day.
- Important Characteristics: Fixed budget,Uncertainty about product compatibility,Preference for guided decisions.



## 3. Customer Job:

What is the customer trying to accomplish?

Customer is trying to do balance various variables such as room size/space for setup, budget constraints, purpose to configure a workspace setup.


## 4. Customer Pains:

What difficulties, risks or frustrations do they experience?

1. More time spent on comparing choices
2. Lack of personalised recommendations
3. Lack of compatibility in their product selection


## 5. Current Alternatives:

How do customers currently approach this problem?

1. Searching across different e-commerce websites
2. Buying individual items over time
3. Estimating measurements manually


## 6. Customer Gains:

What outcomes or improvements do they want?

1. Personalised recommendations based on their needs.
2. Suitable product recommendations based on the customer’s needs and constraints.
3. Confidence that selected products are compatible


## 7. Evidence and Assumptions

### Known or Observed

No formal customer research has been conducted yet. The current problem
definition is based on preliminary observation and requires validation.

### Assumptions

1. Software developers experience difficulty selecting suitable workspace products.
2. Budget, available space and compatibility influence their purchasing decisions.
3. Comparing products across multiple information sources requires significant effort.


## 8. Candidate Problem Statements

### Candidate 1 — Decision Difficulty

Software developers setting up a home workspace need a way to choose suitable products with confidence because evaluating space, budget, intended use, and compatibility together is difficult, which can lead to time-consuming research and unsuitable purchasing decisions.

### Candidate 2 — Fragmented Process

When creating or improving a home workspace, software developers struggle to make an informed product selection because relevant information is spread across e-commerce listings, reviews, videos, and comparison guides, leading to repeated research and uncertainty about the final choice.


### Candidate 3 — Constraint Balancing

Software developers working from home need to assemble a practical workspace while balancing budget, available space, intended use, comfort, and product compatibility, but assessing these constraints across separate products is difficult and may result in poor fit, overspending, or avoidable replacements.





## 9. Final Problem Statement:

Software developers setting up or improving a home workspace need a simpler way to choose suitable products within their budget, available space, and intended use because assessing fit and compatibility across separate products and information sources is difficult, time-consuming, and can result in unsuitable purchases or overspending.



## 10. Value proposition:

For software developers who need to create a practical home workspace but find it difficult to compare suitable products across disconnected sources, DeskCraft is a focused ergonomic workspace shopping platform that helps them discover and choose available products based on their needs and constraints. Unlike browsing unrelated product listings independently, DeskCraft combines focused product discovery with inventory-aware guidance to support a more confident purchasing decision.
