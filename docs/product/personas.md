## 1. Purpose

This document defines DeskCraft’s primary and secondary customer proto-personas. These personas will help guide product decisions based on the users’ goals, challenges and constraints.

## 2. Persona Selection

### Primary customer group

For DeskCraft, the Primary customer group is Software Developers.

### Why this is the primary group

Software developers need to have a good desk setup because they have to stay focused and productive for long periods. Making sure that the desk is set up as well as possible allows for better posture and concentration. As remote work continues to grow in popularity, more and more programmers are setting up their own home offices. Having a dedicated workspace and desk set up in home enviroment can make work more enjoyable.

### Secondary customer group

For DeskCraft, the secondary customer group is students.

### Why they are secondary group

A dedicated home workstation setup is essential for modern students to maintain academic performance, protect their physical health, and minimize cognitive fatigue.


## 3. Primary Persona 

### Background

- Customer type: Software developer
- Relevant situation: For the past 5 years hybrid work culture and work from home became the norm, hence there is a need for workspace setup.
- Workspace situation: Software developers spend considerable amount of hours in a day in their workstation setup, so having a ideal setup based on their need is vital.
- Experience selecting workspace products: More time spent on searching multiple e-commerce platforms for comparing their choices.

### Goals

1. Confidence in selecting their products.
2. Selected products are compatible with one another based on their needs and constraints.

### Challenges

1. Searching across different e-commerce websites
2. Difficulty identifying products that match their individual needs and constraints.


### Important constraints

- Budget: Moderate and varies between customers
- Available space: Limited or medium-sized workspace
- Intended use: Daily professional work
- Product knowledge: Basic to moderate knowledge of workspace products


### Current behaviour
 
1. Searching across different e-commerce websites
2. Buying individual items over time

### Job-to-be-Done

Help software developers to confidently select products compatible with their needs and constraints without making their decision process fragmented in multiple sources.


## 4. Secondary Persona 

### Background

- Customer type: Student
- Relevant situation: In-home workspace setup is becoming a essential requirement for modern students.
- Workspace situation: student users require a workspace setup for completing their academic chores daily.


### Goals

1. Make an informed decison based on their needs.
2. Optimized setup for their limited workspace.


### Challenges

1. Balancing the budget with their needs.
2. Avoiding poor fit.

### Important constraints

- Budget: Fixed and small
- Available space: small
- Intended use: For academic purposes


### Difference from the primary persona

Student users have limited budget and their available setup is quite small compared to software developers. Hence product discovery and selection pool may vary. 

### Job-to-be-Done

Help students to select products for their workspace setup without exceeding their budget and space constraints.


## 5. Assumptions

These proto-personas have not yet been validated through formal customer research.

1. Software developers experience difficulty selecting suitable and compatible workspace products.
2. Software developers compare products across multiple websites before purchasing.
3. Students require workspace products but generally face stricter budget and space constraints.


## 6. Future Validation

These personas can later be checked through customer interviews, surveys, reviews and relevant online discussions.
